When you add an email to a Content Server folder, Enterprise Connect applies a naming convention that has been set by your administrator. If you have the appropriate permissions, you can rename the email after you have added it to Content Server. In addition, depending on how your environment has been configured, you may be able to set email naming conventions. For more information, see Setting Email Naming Rules.

Depending on how your administrator has configured Enterprise Connect, you may encounter some of the following options when adding email to a Content Server container.

Duplicate Email Names

If Enterprise Connect generates an email name that is already assigned to an item in the destination folder, you will be prompted to resolve the naming conflict. This also occurs if you add an email to a folder, and an email with the same name already resides in the destination folder. The new instance of the email will have the same name, with a number added in parentheses at the end to indicate that it is a separate instance. Depending on your configuration, you may be able to rename the email. For more information, see Setting Email Naming Rules.

Providing Metadata for Email

Depending on the configuration of your environment, you may be required to provide metadata when you add an email to a folder in the Enterprise Connect tree. Some metadata may be already provided, and depending on the system configuration, you may be able to edit some boxes as well. For more information about working with Categories and Attributes, see Using Categories.

Attachments

Depending on the configuration of your environment, you may be asked to specify how email and attachments are handled when you add them to the Enterprise Connect tree. You may be asked to do one of the following:

  • Save email and attachments together in the destination folder as a single record.

  • Save email and attachments together in the destination folder as a single record and save a separate instance of the attachments as separate records.

The OpenText Records Management module XReference feature enables you to link from the email to its associated attachments.

 

Tip:
You can view a list of all attachments for a selected email by right-clicking the email, and then clicking List Attachments. In the Attachments dialog box, you can open or download any of the listed attachments.

 
Compound Emails

Depending on how your environment is configured, any email and attachments that you save may be saved as Compound Emails. This means that the email message and any attachments are saved as a single folder in Enterprise Connect. You will notice the following when working with Compound Emails:

  • The icon indicates a Compound Email.

  • Compound Emails only appear in the List Pane in Enterprise Connect and are not visible in the Microsoft Outlook or Explorer Tree Pane.

  • Depending on how your environment has been configured, you may be able to take Compound Email messages and attachments offline. When you take Compound Emails offline, they will be read-only, and you will be able to access any attachments. If your administrator has not enabled offline functionality, Download Offline Version will not be available when you right-click a Compound Email.

  • When you drag a Compound Email to either your Desktop or to another plug-in, the email may be treated either as a regular email or as a Compound Email, depending on how the source server has been configured.

  • You can view attachments for a selected Compound Email in the Reading Pane. From the Reading Pane, you can perform a number of tasks on the attachments, such as editing, opening, or downloading, if you have permission.

     

    Tip:
    You can also view Compound Email attachments by right-clicking a Compound Email, clicking Properties, and then Sub-Items.

     

  • Your administrator specifies what happens when you double-click a Compound Email. Depending on the configuration, the email may either open in Outlook, or a separate web panel will open, containing the email and the attachments. From the web panel, you can open, download, or edit the email or the attachments, depending on your permissions.
