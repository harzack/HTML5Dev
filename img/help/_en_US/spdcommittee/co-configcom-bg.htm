When you configure a community, you can change most of the settings that were specified when the community was created. You can also configure additional settings, such as the components that appear on the Community Homepage.

Configuring a community consists of performing the following tasks to determine its appearance and behavior:

  • Viewing the Community Metrics page.

  • Adding text modules, URL modules, and LiveReports to the Community Homepage.

  • Configuring the community status.

  • Enabling external questions.

  • Allowing users to request or cancel membership to an expert group. For more information about expert groups, see Administering Expert Groups.

  • Specifying a registration question.

  • Changing the community listing.

Setting Up a Community

Perform the following tasks to set up a community:

  • Choose which content appears on the Community Homepage.

    Content modules are components that display small reports on the Community Homepage, for example, upcoming events, recent forum topics, or small pieces of information or functionality. When you configure the community look and feel, you can choose the following features:

    • Specify URLs, images and alternative text to appear on the homepage banner and side bar.

    • Edit the Navigation Menu, including creating custom sidebars.

      The homepage sidebar is a console that appears underneath the Navigation Menu on the Community Homepage. This sidebar can contain any text you want. You can also add links to existing items anywhere in Content Server by adding shortcuts to the Navigation Menu. For more information about shortcuts, see Working with Shortcuts and URLs.

  • Change most of the settings that were specified when the community was created on the Configuration page. For more information, see Creating Communities.

  • Configure what information is to be shown on the Community Digest page.

Setting Role-Based Look and Feel

You can either configure one look and feel for all users, or specify a different look and feel for each of the community's roles. This is known as configuring the role-based look and feel. To implement role-based look and feel, you simply configure the appearance and behavior of the community separately for each defined role, that is, you can display different menu items for some roles while hiding them from others.

Adding Text Modules, URL Modules, and LiveReports to the Community Homepage

Adding text modules, URL modules, and LiveReports to the Community Homepage means that you add content modules that display these pieces of information. A text module is formatted text that may or may not have a title. By adding a URL module, you can point to any Web page that is accessible from your network, such as your organization's home page, and display it in a module on the Community Homepage. For more information about LiveReports, see Working With LiveReports.

 

Note:
Only LiveReports that output a list of nodes are supported on the Community Homepage.

 

Viewing the Community Metrics page

The Community Metrics page displays statistics that indicate the general robustness of a community. Metrics include how many members there are and how actively different elements of the community are being used.

Configuring the Community Status

Configuring the community status consists of the following:

  • Setting the community to online for users or offline, that is, available or unavailable.

  • Setting the community type, that is, public, private, or unlisted.

  • Specifying a mission and purpose.

For more information about setting the community type and specifying a mission and purpose, see Creating Communities.

Enabling External Questions

External questions are asked against a directory in the community directory that the community is listed under. Enabling external questions allows a community to receive and display external questions on its Community Homepage. For more information about questions, see Using Questions and Answers.

Specifying a Registration Question

A registration question is the question that gets asked of users when they request membership to the community.

Changing the Community Listing

When you create a community, you can choose a directory in which to list the community. When you configure the community, you can change the listing or specify additional directories in which to list the community.

Configuring the Community Digest

You configure the community digest by choosing the type of information displayed and what specifics to show for each category. The categories are:

  • Logins

  • New Members

  • Deleted Members

  • Most Viewed Items

The following details are available for each category:

  • Date

  • Name

  • Role

  • Email

  • Phone

The exception to these details is the Most Viewed Items category, for which you can specify only the number of items to display.
