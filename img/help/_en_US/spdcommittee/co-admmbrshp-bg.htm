When you administer membership for a community, you configure who can access its content, and what actions they can perform on the content. This includes setting up and configuring roles, that is, groups of members who perform similar tasks in the community such as guests and facilitators.

Administering membership includes the following tasks:

  • Inviting members.

  • Editing membership.

  • Adding roles.

  • Setting community permissions for a role.

  • Setting sub-item permissions for a role.

  • Accepting or rejecting membership requests.

  • Setting notification for member activity.

  • Sending a welcome email to new members.

  • Accepting or rejecting membership cancellations.

Inviting Members and Editing Membership

You invite users to a community in one of two ways:

  • Individually, in which case users become direct members of the community.

  • As a group, in which case group members become indirect members of the community.

Editing membership consists of changing the role for a user or group, or removing a user or group from the community.

For more information about Content Server users and groups, see Working with Users and Groups.

Adding Roles and Setting Role Permissions

Adding a role consists of naming the role and setting its permissions for the community and its content.

After you add a role, you can change its community permissions at any time, or you can change its permissions for particular items in the community, that is, the community's sub-items.

For more information about permissions, see Managing Permissions.

Accepting or Rejecting Membership Requests

All users can request membership to public and private communities. It is up to the facilitator to accept or reject these requests.

Setting Notification for Member Activity

As with other Content Server items, you can set notification on a community so that you are made aware of any changes, such as when a member adds an item to the library. You can also set up notification for roles in the community. This means that when users of a specific role visit the Notification page, they see the notification reports that you have customized for them. For more information about notifications, see Using Notifications.

Sending a Welcome Email to New Members

Welcome emails introduce users to a community and contain a link to access the community.

Content Server Communities uses the eLink module to send welcome emails. For information about eLink, see Understanding eLink.

Accepting or Rejecting Membership Cancellations

All community members can request to cancel their membership. It is up to the facilitator to accept or reject these requests.
