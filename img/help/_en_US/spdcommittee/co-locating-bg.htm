You can locate communities by browsing or searching the community directory. The community directory lists communities in a hierarchy of directories, which are set up and maintained by Content Server users with system administration privileges. For more information about the community directory, see Using the Community Directory.

Another way to locate communities is by viewing your Personal Frontpage, a customizable page that you can use to receive personalized reports of community-related information, such as lists of new communities. For more information about your Personal Frontpage, see Managing Your View of Communities.

You can also use Content Server Search to locate communities. For more information about searching Content Server, see Searching Content Server.

Communities can be public, private, or unlisted:

  • Public communities allow access to all users, enabling you to view their contents before you join.

  • Private communities only allow you to see their names in the container where they are added. Only members can access the content within.

  • Unlisted communities are not viewable in any way to non-members. Membership to these communities is by invitation only.

Once you have found a public or private community that you would like to join, you can request membership. The facilitator of the community then decides whether to accept or reject your membership request, and may notify you with an email message. For private communities, you must have an existing community member recommend you to the facilitator.

 

Note:
To receive email messages from a facilitator, or any other members of a community, your email address must be specified on your My Profile page. For more information about the My Profile page, see Working with Users.

 

When you join a community, you are assigned a role, which determines the permissions you have in the community, such as member or guest. The actual roles are configured by the facilitator and vary by community.

If you no longer want to be a member of a community, you can request to cancel your membership, which must include a reason for the cancellation. The community's facilitator then decides whether to accept or reject your request.
