A community is an online gathering place that brings together an informal network of people in your organization to share common interests and complementary knowledge, such as salespeople and product-design specialists. Communities enable members to do the following:

  • Generate awareness and share intelligence.

  • Gather and organize information.

  • Find collaborators and opinion leaders.

Communities provide a variety of ways to exchange information with other members and present this information to you in a personalized, newspaper-like Community Homepage. Communities can also contain other communities called sub-communities.

As a member of a community, you can access information supplied by other members in a number of different ways. For example, you can read and comment on blogs posted by other members, search FAQs, or participate in forums. You can add your own blog or collaborate with others in a wiki. A community can also include a lightweight calendar for an at-a-glance view of community events or use functionalities from the Records Management module, if installed. For information on the Records Management module, visit the OpenText Knowledge Center.

For more information about Records Management features and functions, see the Administering Records Management.

Every community is maintained by a facilitator, who is a Content Server user who is responsible for administrating the community, managing its membership, and organizing the information it contains.

Navigating in Communities

In addition to the standard Content Server user-interface controls, communities have their own Navigation Menu sidebar along the left side of the page for access to community-related functions. What you see on this menu depends on your permissions and on menu settings made by the facilitator. If you are the facilitator, for information on editing the Navigation Menu sidebar options, see To Edit Navigation Menu Sidebar Options.
