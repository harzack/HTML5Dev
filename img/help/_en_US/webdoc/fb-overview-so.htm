Faceted browsing is a feature used to browse items in Content Server. Users filter items through predefined groupings, called facets. The Content Filter sidebar panel is used to apply facets to browse to a location in Content Server. Once you have selected the facets you want applied to a container, the Browse View displays the results. The sidebar is turned on and configured by your administrator.

Facets are defined by Content Server, your administrator, and users who have been granted the proper privileges. The administrator grants the privileges to users, known as Knowledge Managers, who will be administering Faceted Browsing. Whether or not the sidebar is available to you is determined by your administrator. Whether or not a facet is available in your sidebar is determined by your administrator or by your Knowledge Managers.

Items are comprised of name, contents, and metadata. Metadata are values that describe an item. Facets are groupings of metadata. The following example refers to the default settings for Content Server, however your administrator may have modified the default settings.

Example

A departmental memo can be described as:

  • Name: Department Memo 5975

  • Content Type: Document

  • Owner: John Smith

The facets in the above example are: Content Type and Owner. The values assigned to these facets are Document and John Smith, respectively. Faceted browsing works by letting you sequentially filter the items in Content Server through the facets to progressively narrow down the items that fit your defined criteria.

If you need to find the item in the example above, but you do not know the item's name, you can use the sidebar to filter all items through Content Type and Owner facets in order to produce a list of items which fit your facet values selections.

If you know that your departmental memos are:

  • always documents created by John Smith, and

  • always saved in the Administration folder,

you can find your departmental memos using the Content Filter sidebar.

First, navigate to the Administration folder. The sidebar will dynamically examine all items, and sub-items, contained within that view. It will then display the facets you can browse to find your document. You can first filter all items through a Type facet whose value is Document. The Browse View will update to display all items in the Administration folder classified by Type as Document. You can then apply a second facet filter to all Document items by selecting the John Smith value from the Owner facet. The result of this will display all Documents created by John Smith which have been saved to the Administration folder.

Examples of facets that are defined by Content Server include: Modified Date, Content Type and Owner. Every time an item is created in Content Server, all facets applicable to that item are given values. Facet values are updated whenever the item is updated, and deleted whenever the item is deleted.

The sidebar is available from most Workspaces and containers in Content Server. If a location does not display the sidebar, or you cannot open the sidebar, then faceted browsing is not available for that location. The sidebar may not be applicable to that particular container, or the items and sub-items of that container may not have any facets to display. In the event that the sidebar is not displayed in a container, Content Server Filtering will be available. For information about Filtering, see Finding Items Using Filtering.

The sidebar is first configured for the entire user population by your administrator. The Knowledge Managers can then apply customizations which are available to the entire user population. Users can then apply their own customizations to the sidebar.

The information displayed in the Browse View is first configured by your administrator. If your administrator has permitted users to customize columns, then you will be able to customize what columns appear in your Browse View. For further information about customizing columns, see To Customize Personal Column Settings.
