Physical Objects lets you store physical items by assigning storage locators. Storage locators are specific sections of an area within a storage facility. For example, an area may contain three rows of shelves, each shelf representing one storage locator.

You can store individual physical items by assigning a storage locator to the physical item or by assigning the physical item to the storage locator. Once you have assigned a storage locator to a physical item, you can remove the assignment. You can also store multiple physical items by assigning a storage locator to physical items that are in a Collection.

Physical Objects provides you with the ability to assign storage locators to specific boxes in transfers. Managed facilities can use the assignment when they receive a transfer to determine where to place the received boxes. For information about assigning boxes to transfers, see Transferring Objects. Before assigning the storage locator, you first must search for it. You can search by facility and area, by total size of selected boxes, and by locators that will fit the largest of the boxes you have selected or all the boxes that you have.

 

Note:
You can only assign storage locators to boxes in transfers that have been received.
