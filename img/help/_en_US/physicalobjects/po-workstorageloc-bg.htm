In Physical Objects, you can manage the storage of physical items for both managed and non-managed facilities by creating storage locators. Storage locators are markers for specific sections in areas of a storage facility. For example, an area of a storage facility can consist of multiple shelving units and a storage locator can refer to one shelf in the shelving unit.

When creating a storage locator for managed facilities, you can specify the frequency at which the locator is accessed, the type of locator, and the dimensions of the locator.

Once you have created storage locators, you can edit the storage locator settings, view the assigned physical items, delete storage locators that do not have physical items assigned, and recalculate available storage space. When you recalculate storage space, you are recalculating the storage space for all locators assigned specified facility and area codes. Recalculating storage space lets you ensure the accuracy of available free-space. You can also verify the contents of a locator by uploading a document that contains the data collected via a remote scanner. A table displays the results, including the facility, the area, the locator, the unique ID, and the status of the verification. If the locator and the unique ID of an item are correct, but the item has not been assigned to the locator, a check box displays; you can update the item to create this assignment. You can save this information to a log file; which is named verifylocator_date/time, where date/time is the date and time when the log file was generated.
