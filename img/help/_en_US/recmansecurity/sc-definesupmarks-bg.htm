Supplemental markings are non-hierarchical. They are implemented to provide access to the metadata of individual objects. They are also used to filter searches and provide users with lists of objects they can see based on their assigned supplemental marking.

Supplemental markings have multiple-attribute codes that are attached to individual objects. Typically, this type of filtering is performed before a check of the object's Access Control List, as is the case with security clearance levels.
