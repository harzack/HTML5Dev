Moderating a forum consists of overseeing which posts will actually appear in the forum. In this way, you can keep control over the content of the forum and prevent inappropriate posts.

When you moderate a forum that requires approval of all posts, you view the list of pending posts, and then accept or reject each one. When you reject a post, a rejection message is sent to the contributor. You can edit this message so that it explains the reasons for the rejection.

When you moderate a forum that does not require approval of all posts, you can reject any inappropriate posts that appear, but you do not have to approve posts for them to appear in the forum.

 

Note:
To enable moderating on a forum, select a moderator in the Forum Settings page.

 

Pinning Forum Topics

Moderators can also pin forum topics that are inside a community. When you pin a forum topic, it appears with the other pinned topics, fixed to the top of the list of topics that users see when they view the forum. Pinned forum topics are more easily seen by those who access the forum and are a useful way to avoid duplicate forum topics.

You can also view a list of a forum's moderators. For more information about listing contributors, see Subscribing to Forums.

 

Note:
For forums inside communities, facilitators have the same privileges as the moderators.

 

For more information about communities, see Using Communities.
