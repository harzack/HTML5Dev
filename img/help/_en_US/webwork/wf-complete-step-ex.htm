Imagine that John Smith is a manager and one of his common tasks is reviewing and commenting on sales trip reports. When sales representatives return from a sales trip, they are required to submit their expenses and a report that summarizes the trip through an email-enabled Workflow named Trip Review Workflow. John is assigned to the Review Trip Documents step, where he must review the expense and trip reports, and then approve or reject the step.

Bob, a sales representative, has just returned from a trip to the mid-Atlantic region. After preparing his expense and trip reports, Bob initiates the Trip Review Workflow, names it Bob's Mid-Atlantic Trip Reports, and attaches his reports. When Workflow processing reaches the Review Trip Documents step, Content Server sends an email message to John. See Figure 1-3.

Figure 1-3: Review Trip Documents Step Email Message

When John opens the message, he can review the instructions and then review the reports, which are included in the message as attachments. If he wants to see more details about the step or the Workflow in Content Server, John can click the Workflow Step Page link. If he determines that he is ready to approve or reject the reports, he can finish work on the step by clicking a disposition button. John decides to reject the report and attach a version of the trip summary so that Bob can review his comments and resubmit the reports; therefore, he clicks Reject. The email program creates the a response message, which John modifies to include his comments, and then attaches an updated version of the trip summary. See Figure 1-4.

Figure 1-4: Step Rejection Email Message

 

Notes:

  • The message recipient is an automatically generated virtual user that represents the current instance of the Workflow step. Do not modify or send other messages to this message recipient address.

  • After you send the automatically generated email message created by clicking the Send On or a disposition button, the Action Requested email message that includes the button you clicked is displayed again. Do not click a button in the Action Requested email message again unless you are sure there was a problem processing your last response.

 

When Content Server receives John's message, it copies his version of the trip report to the Workflow Attachments folder and adds his message from Bob to the step's Comments section. After processing John's response message, it returns the a confirmation message to John so that he knows that the step was successfully processed. For example, see Figure 1-5.
