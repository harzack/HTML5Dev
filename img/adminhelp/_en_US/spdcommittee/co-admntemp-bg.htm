Community templates provide a default structure and content for communities. You add and configure community templates to suit certain purposes, for example, a product development group, so that it is easier to configure newly created communities. Community templates are stored in the community templates volume, which makes them available system-wide. You can organize your community templates in the volume by storing them in community template folders.

When you add a community template, you configure it just as you would a new community, including configuring the look and feel, and adding URLs to the community homepage. You can also configure the community template just as you would when facilitating a community, including administering membership, configuring subscriptions, and administering expert groups. For more information about configuring communities, see Facilitating a Community.

 

Note:
When adding a community template, you must create a facilitator role with full permissions so that one or more users can manage the communities created with the template.

Forums can be added to a community template if you start creating a forum in another container in Content Server by selecting the community template as the target destination in which to place the forum in the Create In box.

 

Instead of creating all new community templates, you can add a demo template to serve as the basis for all future templates. The demo template is preconfigured to provide users with the basic characteristics for the community templates and communities in your organization.
